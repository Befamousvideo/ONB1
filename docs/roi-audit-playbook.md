# StorenTech ROI Audit Playbook

Use this guide for ONB1, the website chatbot, and voice agents whenever a prospect is ready to move from intake into a paid ROI audit.

## Positioning

- This is not a generic discovery call.
- The ROI Audit is a fee-based StorenTech consulting step that identifies the highest-value automation to solve first.
- The typical fee range is `$500-$1000`.
- That fee may be credited toward the first service engagement when approved.

## Core Outcome

Every ROI Audit should end with:

- the top 1 to 2 pain points
- the best first automation to deploy
- the baseline numbers needed to prove value
- a 30-day measurement plan
- a clear reason to delay more advanced AI until quick wins are proven

## Appointment Windows

- In-person ROI audit appointments:
  - Monday through Thursday
  - 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM local time
  - Exceptions require approval
- Voice call ROI audit appointments:
  - Monday through Thursday
  - 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM local time
  - Exceptions require owner Vincent approval

## Shared Framing Statement

Use language like:

> This is not about selling every AI tool available. The goal is to identify the 1 or 2 automation opportunities most likely to produce measurable value first, then decide whether it makes sense to go deeper.

## Four Questions The Audit Must Answer

1. Where is this business leaking money or time right now?
2. Which problem is painful enough, frequent enough, and measurable enough to fix first?
3. What automation would solve that problem with the least friction?
4. How will StorenTech prove ROI after 30 days?

## Typical Call Structure

- Recommended duration: 35 to 50 minutes
- Sequence:
  - set the frame
  - run universal ROI questions
  - run category-specific module
  - score the likely opportunities
  - confirm baseline metrics and 30-day plan
  - close on the first pilot recommendation

## Universal ROI Audit Modules

### A. Business Priority and Pressure
- Ask about:
  - the most important 90-day outcome
  - the biggest frustration or daily bottleneck
  - the one issue they would solve first
- Usually signals:
  - missed leads / calls = voice agent, chatbot, missed-call automation
  - slow follow-up = CRM follow-up and estimate reminders
  - repetitive admin = workflow automation
  - privacy concerns = local/private AI track

### B. Customer Entry Points
- Ask about:
  - first contact channels
  - weekly volume of calls / leads / forms / messages
  - after-hours demand
  - what happens when nobody answers
  - actual response speed
- Usually signals:
  - high phone volume + missed calls = AI receptionist / voice agent
  - high traffic + weak conversion = website chatbot / guided intake
  - slow response = instant follow-up automation

### C. Workflow From Inquiry to Cash
- Ask about:
  - the full workflow from first contact to completed sale or service
  - where handoffs slow down
  - where manual chasing, copy-paste, or re-entry happens
- Usually signals:
  - intake bottleneck = intake assistant
  - scheduling bottleneck = booking / dispatch / reminder automation
  - quote bottleneck = estimate follow-up
  - status bottleneck = automated notifications or support assistant

### D. Time Burden and Labor Waste
- Ask about:
  - repetitive tasks consuming staff time
  - expensive people doing low-value admin work
  - approximate weekly hours and labor cost
- Usually signals:
  - owner doing admin = strong quick-win ROI case
  - office staff buried in calls = voice / scheduling automation
  - repetitive FAQs = chatbot or knowledge assistant

### E. Revenue Leakage
- Ask about:
  - lost deals, stale quotes, abandoned inquiries, no-shows, lost renewals
  - average customer, job, or order value
  - approximate gross profit contribution
  - old leads or quotes not followed up
- Usually signals:
  - stale quotes = follow-up automation
  - weak retention = reactivation automation
  - no-shows = reminder and reconfirmation flows

### F. Systems, Tools, and Data Readiness
- Ask about:
  - CRM, calendar, phone, POS, ecommerce, ERP, email tools, service software
  - where customer data lives
  - where staff re-enter data manually
  - whether reporting exists already
- Usually signals:
  - disconnected systems = integration opportunity
  - heavy manual re-entry = workflow automation
  - weak reporting = start with automations that generate clean measurable logs

### G. Security, Privacy, and Strategic Positioning
- Ask about:
  - customer data sensitivity
  - proprietary processes
  - preference for local/private AI or controlled environments
- Usually signals:
  - high concern = position OpenClaw, local models, DGX Spark, private databases
  - moderate concern = hybrid path
  - low concern = practical cloud-connected automations first

### H. Pilot Readiness
- Ask about:
  - which improvement would create the fastest visible win
  - which change the team would actually adopt
  - who owns the project internally
  - what can be measured before and after 30 days
- Usually signals:
  - easy adoption + measurable metric = ideal first pilot
  - complex change + weak data = not the first project

## Category-Specific Audit Modules

### Service Industry
- Extra focus:
  - urgent calls vs scheduled work
  - missed calls and delayed responses
  - estimate follow-up
  - scheduling and dispatch load
  - renewals and recurring service reminders
- Common first automation:
  - AI voice receptionist, estimate follow-up, or scheduling automation
- Best 30-day metrics:
  - calls answered or recovered
  - booked appointments
  - estimate close rate
  - admin hours saved

### Retail, Restaurants, and Hospitality
- Extra focus:
  - bookings, reservations, takeout, and special-order handling
  - repetitive guest questions
  - review / loyalty follow-up
  - social and message-response load
- Common first automation:
  - reservation / order voice agent, FAQ bot, or reactivation automation
- Best 30-day metrics:
  - bookings captured
  - repeat visits
  - response speed
  - staff time saved

### Online Retail
- Extra focus:
  - conversion rate
  - cart abandonment
  - support volume
  - product-finder friction
  - repeat purchase rate
- Common first automation:
  - conversational sales assistant, cart recovery, or support bot
- Best 30-day metrics:
  - conversion rate
  - recovered-cart revenue
  - ticket deflection
  - average order value

### Manufacturing / RFQ-Driven B2B
- Extra focus:
  - RFQ arrival and quote turnaround
  - order-status questions
  - technical knowledge burden
  - downtime or supplier delays
  - IP sensitivity
- Common first automation:
  - RFQ intake assistant, quote-routing, or support knowledge assistant
- Best 30-day metrics:
  - quote turnaround time
  - quote win rate
  - support hours saved
  - order-status inquiry reduction

### Facilities, Clubs, Schools, and Booking Businesses
- Extra focus:
  - booking friction
  - no-shows
  - repetitive member/guest questions
  - renewals and reactivation
  - event and class admin load
- Common first automation:
  - booking/waitlist automation, member concierge, or renewal campaigns
- Best 30-day metrics:
  - bookings captured
  - no-show reduction
  - renewal lift
  - admin hours saved

### Specialty / High-Trust Businesses
- Extra focus:
  - intake friction
  - document collection
  - no-shows
  - routine status questions
  - sensitivity of records
- Common first automation:
  - intake assistant, reminder automation, or FAQ/status bot with human handoff
- Best 30-day metrics:
  - intake completion time
  - consultation bookings
  - no-show reduction
  - document processing time saved

## Opportunity Scoring

At the end of the audit, score each candidate opportunity from 1 to 5 on:

- pain severity
- frequency
- financial impact
- ease of implementation
- ease of measurement
- owner urgency

The first project is usually the one with the best mix of high pain, high frequency, low friction, and a visible 30-day win.

## 30-Day ROI Proof Model

### Baseline Before Implementation

Collect:

- volume of leads, calls, inquiries, orders, or bookings
- average response time
- conversion rate
- no-show or cancellation rate
- staff hours spent on the target task
- average ticket value
- estimated gross profit per sale, job, or member
- missed opportunities per month

### Measure Again After 30 Days

Use the same metrics and simple math:

- Time saved = hours saved x hourly labor value
- Revenue gained = additional conversions x gross profit per sale
- Losses avoided = fewer no-shows / cancellations / errors x cost per event
- ROI = `(total monthly benefit - monthly project cost) / monthly project cost`

Use gross profit whenever possible, not just top-line revenue.

## Closing Questions

- Which of these problems feels most urgent to solve first?
- Which one would your team adopt most easily?
- Which one can StorenTech measure cleanly within 30 days?
- What would success look like in actual numbers?
- Who on your team would own this internally?
- Are you open to starting with the highest-ROI quick win first, then expanding into more advanced automation later?
